Mesh Network Satellites - The Future of Communication

The deployment of Low Earth Orbit (LEO) mesh network satellite systems, most notably SpaceX's Starlink, represents a significant shift in global internet infrastructure. This project explores how LEO constellations use inter-satellite links and distributed mesh routing to deliver low-latency broadband connectivity to rural and remote communities that conventional fibre and mobile networks have consistently failed to reach. Alongside this potential to bridge the digital divide, the technology raises important risks and challenges, including variable real-world performance, the long-term sustainability of an increasingly congested orbital environment, and the impact of satellite trails on ground-based astronomy. By examining the social, ethical, and technical implications of LEO satellite broadband — and the choices made by engineers, companies, and policymakers in its deployment — this project aims to develop a balanced and evidence-based understanding of how this technology can be integrated responsibly alongside existing infrastructure to maximise public benefit while minimising lasting harm.
